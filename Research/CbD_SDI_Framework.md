# CbD SDI Framework
*Communicate by Design | Compiled: 2026-03-29 | Version 1.0*

---

## Purpose and Use

This document is the research-backed framework governing how Communicate by Design designs and labels Specially Designed Instruction (SDI) components across all product lines. It exists because SDI is a legal requirement under IDEA — not a synonym for "extra help" or "modified materials." Without a principled framework, CbD products may provide differentiation without ensuring that differentiation rises to the level of SDI, and IEP teams using CbD materials may not know which components constitute legally documentable SDI.

**This framework answers three operational questions:**

1. **What is SDI under IDEA?** (Legal definition and three adaptation types)
2. **Which CbD product components constitute SDI?** (And which are accommodations)
3. **What documentation evidence does a team need?** (Data trail)

It applies to all CbD text-based product lines. Product design must distinguish SDI from accommodation — the distinction has legal and instructional consequences.

**IEP goal stems are documented separately.** The model language for writing measurable annual goals connected to CbD units — including IDEA measurability requirements, the accommodation vs. modification distinction, observable verb guidance, and goal stems by unit and skill type — is in `Research/CbD_IEP_Goal_Ecosystem.md` and the quick reference `_Operations/memory/iep_goal_ecosystem_reference.md`. This file focuses on the product design question: which components constitute SDI and how must they be identified.

## Part 1: The Legal and Research Foundation

### Layer 1: IDEA Definition of Specially Designed Instruction

**Primary source: IDEA 2004, 20 U.S.C. § 1401(29); 34 CFR §300.39**

The federal definition of Specially Designed Instruction:

> "Specially designed instruction means adapting, as appropriate to the needs of an eligible child under this part, the **content, methodology, or delivery of instruction** to address the unique needs of the child that result from the child's disability and to ensure access of the child to the general curriculum, so that the child can meet the educational standards within the jurisdiction of the public agency that apply to all children."
> — 34 CFR §300.39(b)(3)

Three key distinctions from this definition:

1. **SDI is not an accommodation.** An accommodation changes how a student accesses or demonstrates learning without changing the standard or the instructional method (e.g., extended time, preferential seating, read-aloud as a test accommodation). SDI changes the *instruction itself* — how content is taught, what methodology is used, or how the instruction is delivered.

2. **SDI targets the unique needs that result from the disability.** Not all differentiation is SDI. A student who receives a V2 text because the team has identified a specific reading-disability-related need is receiving SDI. A student who receives V2 text because the teacher ran out of V1 copies is receiving an accommodation at best.

3. **SDI must appear in the IEP.** SDI is legally required to be documented in the IEP, including who provides it, where, how often, and toward what measurable annual goal. Products that provide SDI-quality materials without connecting them to IEP documentation leave teams legally exposed.

### Layer 2: SDI vs. Accommodation — The Operational Distinction

This is the most common source of confusion in practice. The distinction matters because:
- Accommodations do not have to be in the IEP's SDI section (though they appear elsewhere)
- SDI must appear in the IEP and be provided by a qualified special educator
- If a student fails to make progress, the IEP team must examine whether SDI was implemented with fidelity — not just whether accommodations were in place

| | **Accommodation** | **Specially Designed Instruction** |
|--|-------------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Changes** | Format or setting | Methodology or content delivery |
| **Standard** | Unchanged | Unchanged (but access pathway is adapted) |
| **Who provides** | Any staff | Qualified special educator (or related service provider) |
| **IEP location** | Accommodations section | SDI/Services section |
| **CbD example** | Extended time to complete the Perspective Comparison | Systematic pre-teaching of Tier 2 vocabulary using ALgS before reading |
| **CbD example** | Preferential seating near the projector | V3 adapted text with embedded vocabulary access and partner read-aloud protocol |

**The CbD products provide the materials for SDI; the special educator and IEP team provide the decision-making about whether and how to use them as SDI.**

### Layer 3: Endrew F. and the Meaningful Benefit Standard

**Primary source: Endrew F. v. Douglas County School District (2017), 580 U.S. 386**

The Supreme Court's unanimous decision in *Endrew F.* elevated the FAPE standard: an IEP must be "reasonably calculated to enable a child to make progress appropriate in light of the child's circumstances." The Court explicitly rejected the previous "some educational benefit" floor, stating that this standard offers "no guidance" for what constitutes an appropriate education.

**What this means for CbD SDI design:**

- SDI components must be designed to produce measurable progress, not just access
- "Progress appropriate in light of the child's circumstances" means the bar is individualized — but it is not a low bar
- IEP goals connected to CbD units must be ambitious enough to represent genuine growth
- A student who completes V3 every year without growth toward V2 is not receiving an appropriately ambitious program

**The CbD alignment:** IEP goal stems in CbD units are designed with Endrew F. in mind — they target the standard with measurable criteria and assume forward progress, not maintenance.

### Layer 4: HLP Research — SDI Design

**Primary source: McLeskey et al. (2017) — CEC High-Leverage Practices in Special Education**

The HLPs most directly relevant to SDI design in CbD products:

| HLP | Name | SDI Application |
|-----|------|----------------|
| **HLP 11** | Identify and prioritize long- and short-term learning goals | IEP goal stems in CbD units must connect to both annual goals and lesson-level objectives |
| **HLP 12** | Systematically design instruction toward a specific learning goal | Every SDI component traces to a specific measurable goal — not "exposure to grade-level content" |
| **HLP 13** | Adapt curriculum tasks and materials | V1/V2/V3 differentiation IS HLP 13 — systematic adaptation of the same curriculum materials |
| **HLP 16** | Use explicit instruction | Vocabulary Preview, CER modeling, annotation modeling — these are explicit instruction sequences that constitute SDI |
| **HLP 22** | Provide positive and constructive feedback | The SDI data tracker (session tracker + rubric) creates the feedback loop |

## Part 2: What CbD Components Constitute SDI

### SDI-Level Components (Must Connect to IEP)

These components require a qualified special educator's decision to use and connect to an IEP annual goal:

| Component | SDI Type | Why It Qualifies |
|-----------|----------|-----------------|
| **V2 or V3 adapted text** | Content + Methodology | Adapts both what the student reads and how the text is presented; disability-related need required |
| **Vocabulary Pre-Teaching (Vocabulary Preview Routine)** | Methodology | Systematic explicit instruction; not incidental vocabulary support |
| **Communication Access Packet (CAP)** | Methodology + Delivery | Provides the AAC-specific vocabulary access strategy; requires SLP collaboration to implement |
| **AAC Response Pathway** | Delivery | Changes how the student demonstrates learning; requires IEP documentation of communication modality |
| **SDI Activity Differentiation** | Methodology | V3 choice/cloze format combined with partner read-aloud is a systematic methodology change |
| **Partner Read-Aloud Protocol** | Delivery | When formalized as the consistent delivery mode in the IEP, qualifies as SDI delivery |

### Accommodation-Level Components (Do Not Require IEP SDI Section)

These components are good instructional practice and may be listed in the accommodations section of the IEP, but do not rise to the level of SDI:

| Component | Accommodation Type |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Symbol Cards PDF | Visual support accommodation |
| Extended time for any activity | Testing/completion accommodation |
| Read-aloud as a test accommodation | Testing accommodation (different from partner read-aloud as primary delivery) |
| Preferential seating or grouping | Environmental accommodation |
| V1 text with sentence starters only | Minimal scaffolding — may not require SDI documentation |

## Part 4: SDI Documentation Trail

### Minimum Documentation for SDI Use of CbD Products

When a CbD unit is used as SDI, the IEP team needs a documentation trail that demonstrates:

1. **Identification of need:** The IEP identifies a specific disability-related need that this SDI addresses (e.g., "phonological processing deficit impacting reading comprehension of grade-level informational text")

2. **Goal alignment:** The IEP annual goal reflects the standard targeted by the unit, with measurable criteria

3. **SDI description:** The IEP's SDI section names the specific adaptation (e.g., "adapted text with AAC vocabulary support and partner-read protocol") and connects it to the identified need

4. **Implementation fidelity:** The Communication Access Session Tracker (or equivalent) documents that the AAC vocabulary was pre-programmed, the CAP was shared with the SLP, and partner support was implemented as designed

5. **Progress data:** Data collected at each unit activity (rubric scores, accuracy counts, AAC output tallies) that can be reported at IEP progress reporting dates

### What the CAP Provides for Documentation

The Communication Access Packet is designed to serve as part of the SDI documentation trail. It provides:
- The vocabulary list that was sent to the SLP for pre-programming (timestamp of delivery = implementation evidence)
- The AAC-specific instructional strategy embedded in each activity
- The Session Tracker for per-session data collection

## Part 5: QC Checklist — Pre-Build

**Gate 1: SDI Component Identification**
- [ ] Which components in this unit constitute SDI? (Documented in unit introduction)
- [ ] Which components are accommodations? (Listed separately)
- [ ] Are SDI components labeled as requiring IEP documentation?

**Gate 2: IEP Goal Stems**
- [ ] Goal stems present for this unit's skill type (academic + AAC)?
- [ ] Goal stems follow IDEA structure per `Research/CbD_IEP_Goal_Ecosystem.md`?
- [ ] "Understand" / "know" / "learn" NOT used in any goal stem?

**Gate 3: SDI vs. Accommodation Distinction**
- [ ] Is the distinction between SDI and accommodation explained in the unit teacher guidance?
- [ ] Are V2/V3 texts labeled as "SDI-level adaptation requiring IEP documentation" (not just "differentiation")?
- [ ] Is the CAP explicitly identified as the SDI delivery tool for the AAC component?
