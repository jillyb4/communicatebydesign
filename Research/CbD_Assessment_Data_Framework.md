# CbD Assessment and Data Framework
*Communicate by Design | Compiled: 2026-03-29 | Version 1.0*

---

## Purpose and Use

This document is the research-backed framework governing how Communicate by Design designs data collection tools, mastery criteria, and progress monitoring systems embedded in its products. It exists because data collection is not optional in SDI — IDEA requires measurable annual goals and progress reporting. Without a principled approach to what data is collected, how mastery is defined, and who collects it, CbD products may provide strong instruction without providing the evidence trail IEP teams need to demonstrate FAPE.

**This framework answers four operational questions:**

1. **What counts as evidence of learning in a CbD lesson?** (Valid data types)
2. **What constitutes mastery for CbD ELA + AAC goals?** (Mastery criteria)
3. **Who collects data and how often?** (Data roles)
4. **How do teams use CbD data for IEP progress reporting?** (Reporting)

It applies to all CbD text-based product lines. Data system design is not an afterthought — it must be built into each unit at the same time as the instructional activities.

## Part 1: The Research Foundation

### Layer 1: IDEA Progress Monitoring Requirements

**Primary source: IDEA 2004, 34 CFR §300.320(a)(3)**

IDEA requires IEP annual goals to include:
1. A description of *how the child's progress toward the annual goal will be measured*
2. *When periodic reports on progress* will be provided to parents

This means every CbD unit needs an embedded data collection mechanism. "The student completed the activity" is not progress monitoring data. "The student correctly identified 3 of 4 perspective differences with textual evidence on the Perspective Comparison chart, across 2 consecutive administrations" is.

**The specificity requirement:** Progress data must be specific enough that a different teacher reading the data can determine whether the student met criteria. Rubric scores, accuracy counts, symbol output tallies — these are specific. "Good participation" is not.

### Layer 2: Communication Matrix and AAC Assessment

**Primary source: Rowland (2011) — Communication Matrix; Beukelman & Mirenda (2013)**

The Communication Matrix (Rowland, 2011) is the most widely used assessment tool for individuals with complex communication needs. It describes seven levels of communicative behavior, from pre-intentional to abstract symbolic, and tracks progress across four functions: refusing, obtaining, social interaction, and information.

**The relevance for CbD:** AAC goals embedded in CbD units must be calibrated to the student's current communication level — it is not appropriate to set a goal for symbolic AAC output for a student who is communicating at the pre-symbolic level. The Communication Matrix informs:
- Which AAC goal type is appropriate (symbol selection, multi-symbol utterance, partner-prompted, spontaneous)
- What level of independence to expect (full independence, with modeling, with choice prompt)
- What constitutes genuine progress vs. maintenance

**The SETT Framework (Zabala, 2005):**

The SETT (Student, Environment, Task, Tools) framework informs CbD data collection design by insisting that data is collected in the context of the environment and task — not just on the student in isolation.

| SETT Component | Data Collection Implication |
|---------------|----------------------------|
| Student | What does the student currently do without support? (baseline) |
| Environment | Does the data collection tool work in the actual classroom setting? |
| Task | Is data collected on the specific task the student is being asked to do (not a generalized measure)? |
| Tools | Is the AAC tool accessible when data is collected? Was vocabulary pre-programmed? |

A data point collected without a functioning communication tool is not a valid assessment of the student's capacity.

### Layer 3: Dynamic Assessment

**Primary source: Ukrainetz, Harpell, Walsh & Coyle (2000); Feuerstein (1979)**

Dynamic assessment (test-teach-retest) is the assessment model most aligned to SDI: it measures the student's learning trajectory, not just their current performance. In a dynamic model:
1. **Test (baseline):** What can the student do without support?
2. **Teach (SDI):** Provide explicit, systematic instruction
3. **Retest:** What can the student do after instruction?

The gain score — not the absolute performance — is the evidence of SDI impact. A student who moves from 0 of 5 to 3 of 5 correct has made more meaningful progress than a student who stays at 5 of 5.

**CbD implementation:** The Session Tracker collects pre/during/post data within each unit. The pre/post difference — not just the final score — is the reportable progress metric.

### Layer 4: Mastery Criteria Research

**Primary source: Alberto & Troutman (2022) — Applied Behavior Analysis; Browder et al. (2008)**

Research on mastery criteria for students with disabilities identifies the components of a valid mastery criterion:

1. **Performance criterion:** How accurately? (e.g., 80%, 4 of 5, 3 consecutive correct)
2. **Consistency criterion:** Across how many trials? (single trial mastery is not mastery)
3. **Generalization criterion:** Across how many partners, settings, or contexts?

The commonly cited "80% accuracy across 3 consecutive trials" represents research consensus for academic skills. For communication goals, generalization across 2+ partners is typically required before mastery is declared — a student who uses AAC with one familiar teacher but not with others has not generalized the skill.

**The critical principle for AAC + literacy goals:** Mastery requires stability across time AND partners. A single high-performance session followed by regression is not mastery. CbD data tools are designed to collect data across multiple sessions and (when possible) multiple partners.

## Part 2: Data Types in CbD Products

### Type 1: Rubric-Based Scoring

Used for: response activities (Perspective Comparison, CER, ESR, Source Analysis)

CbD rubrics are 3-level (does not meet / approaching / meets) and criterion-referenced — the criteria align to the ELA standard, not to peer comparison.

**Design rules for CbD rubrics:**
- Each level must describe observable student behavior, not evaluative labels
- The "meets" criterion must reflect the grade-level standard — it is not lowered for SDI versions
- The rubric must be usable by a para with no content expertise — behavioral descriptions only
- Rubric scores are reported as numbers (0/1/2) for aggregation across trials

**Version note:** The rubric is the SAME across V1/V2/V3. The version determines how the student accesses the task; the rubric determines whether they met the standard. A V3 student who selects a correct answer in choice/cloze format is scored the same as a V1 student who writes a full sentence — both demonstrated understanding at the meets criterion.

---

### Type 2: Event Recording (Accuracy Count)

Used for: AAC symbol output, annotation accuracy, vocabulary identification

Event recording counts the number of correct responses out of total opportunities. This is the most straightforward data type for IEP progress reporting.

**Format:** [Correct responses] / [Total opportunities] = [Percentage]

**Example:** Student selected correct Fitzgerald Key category for 7 of 10 vocabulary words = 70%

**Minimum trials for reliable data:** 3–5 per session. Single-trial data is not reportable.

---

### Type 3: Permanent Product (Work Sample)

Used for: completed graphic organizers, annotation guides, CER frames, response sheets

Work samples are the most legally defensible data type because they exist independently of the observer. A completed Perspective Comparison chart with the student's responses is evidence that persists.

**Design rule:** All CbD response activities are designed as permanent products — the student record stays on the paper/document and is reviewable. No oral-only responses without a data collection form.

---

### Type 4: Communication Session Tracker

The CbD Communication Access Session Tracker is designed for per-session AAC communication data collection. It captures:
- Which vocabulary was modeled by the partner
- How many times the student produced AAC output (any modality)
- Whether multi-symbol utterances occurred
- Partner wait time rating (did partner wait 5 seconds?)
- Notes on spontaneous communication

This tracker is the data tool for AAC communication goals — separate from the ELA content rubric.

## Part 3: Mastery Criteria by Skill Type

**These are the default criteria built into CbD IEP goal stems. Teams may adjust upward; CbD recommends against adjusting below 70%.**

| Skill Type | Academic Criterion | AAC Communication Criterion |
|-----------|-------------------|----------------------------|
| Close Reading / Annotation | 80% correct annotation codes across 3 consecutive sessions | 4 of 5 opportunities with symbol output; 2+ consecutive sessions |
| Author's Purpose / Perspective | 2 perspectives identified with textual evidence, 3 of 4 trials | 2+ symbol sequence response to perspective question, 4 of 5 opportunities |
| CER | Claim + 2 evidence pieces aligned, 4 of 5 trials | CER frame completed via AAC output, 3 of 5 trials |
| Text Structure | 3 of 4 text structure elements labeled correctly, 3 consecutive trials | Cause/effect or problem/solution identified via AAC, 4 of 5 opportunities |
| Sourcing / Corroboration | 3 of 4 sources correctly rated with justification, 2 consecutive trials | Source strength indicated via AAC with justification, 3 of 5 opportunities |

**Generalization requirement:** Data collected with at least 2 different partners before mastery is reported on the IEP progress note. One session = trend. Three sessions across 2 partners = mastery.

## Part 4: Data Roles

### Who Collects What

| Role | Data Collected | Tool |
|------|---------------|------|
| Special educator | Rubric scores on response activities; session-level accuracy counts | CbD rubrics; event recording form in unit |
| Paraprofessional | Communication Session Tracker; symbol output tallies | Session Tracker in CAP |
| SLP | Vocabulary access data; pre-programming confirmation | CAP delivery date; vocabulary checklist |
| Student (when possible) | Self-assessment on rubric | Self-assessment included in Student Print Materials |

**The key principle:** Data collection must be built into the activity — not added after. An activity that requires a separate data sheet the teacher forgot to bring is an activity with no data.

**CbD design standard:** Every rubric-scored activity in a CbD unit includes the rubric ON the student's response document. The teacher scores while reviewing the student's work — not as a separate step.

## Part 5: Progress Reporting

### Connecting CbD Data to IEP Progress Reports

IEP progress is typically reported quarterly. A team using a CbD unit across 4–6 weeks of instruction should have sufficient data to report progress on any goal aligned to the unit.

**The progress note minimum:**
1. Current performance level: "[Student] is currently scoring X of 5 / X% on [skill type] activities using [V1/V2/V3] materials and [description of supports]"
2. Trend: "This represents [increase / maintenance / decline] from [baseline score] at the start of the unit"
3. Next step: "The team will [continue current version / move to V2 / add independence criterion] based on this data"

**The non-negotiable:** Progress notes must reference the goal. "Student enjoyed the lesson" is not a progress note. "Student met the 80% accuracy criterion on 2 of 3 data collection sessions but has not yet met the 3-consecutive-sessions criterion" is a progress note.

## Part 6: QC Checklist — Pre-Build

**Gate 1: Data Tool Completeness**
- [ ] Rubric included for every scored response activity?
- [ ] Rubric is on the student's response document (not a separate sheet)?
- [ ] Rubric uses observable behavior descriptions at each level (not evaluative labels)?
- [ ] Rubric is identical across V1/V2/V3?
- [ ] Session Tracker / event recording form included in CAP?

**Gate 2: Mastery Criteria**
- [ ] IEP goal stems include a performance criterion (accuracy/frequency)?
- [ ] IEP goal stems include a consistency criterion (number of trials)?
- [ ] Generalization across 2+ partners referenced in goal stem or teacher guidance?
- [ ] Default mastery criterion ≥70%?

**Gate 3: Data Role Clarity**
- [ ] Teacher document specifies who collects what data?
- [ ] Communication Session Tracker assigned to paraprofessional in teacher guidance?
- [ ] SLP vocabulary pre-programming confirmation step documented?
