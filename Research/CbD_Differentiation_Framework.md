# CbD Differentiation Framework
*Communicate by Design | Compiled: 2026-03-29 | Version 2.0 — Revised per Session 19*

---

## Purpose and Use

This document is the research-backed framework governing how Communicate by Design differentiates instructional materials across text complexity versions. It exists because differentiation is the mechanism through which CbD delivers on its core commitment — that the standard does not change, only the access pathway. Without a principled, evidence-based methodology, version differences drift from "scaffolded access" toward "reduced expectation," which undermines the product's instructional validity and its legal defensibility under IDEA's access mandate.

**This framework answers five operational questions:**

1. **What is the V1/V2/V3 model?** (Version definitions and design intent)
2. **What changes across versions?** (The three differentiation mechanisms)
3. **What never changes?** (The locked constants — including HLPs)
4. **Is this an accommodation or a modification?** (IDEA legal distinction)
5. **How do we know a V3 student is still meeting the standard?** (The access test)

It applies to all Communicate by Design text-based product lines. The Lexile bands and word count targets shift by product line, but the differentiation logic is the same.

**Cross-reference:** Every new text-based product line should be evaluated against Section 5 (Product Line Application) and the Section 8 QC Checklist before build begins. The Communication Access Framework governs how CbD's AAC overlay applies across the full product ecosystem.

## Part 1: The Research Foundation

CbD differentiation sits at the intersection of three evidence bases. Every version decision must be defensible across all three simultaneously.

### Layer 1: IDEA — Accommodation vs. Modification

**Primary source: IDEA 2004, 34 CFR §300.160; §300.39; Parent Center Hub (aligned with IDEA definitions)**

This is the legal foundation. Every CbD differentiation decision must be defensible as an *accommodation*, not a *modification*.

**The legal distinction:**

| | Accommodation | Modification |
|--|---------------|--------------|
| **Definition** | Same standard and content expectations; different access method | Different or reduced standard; lowered content expectation |
| **IDEA legal status** | Permitted for all students with IEPs | Only "alternate academic achievement standards" permitted — exclusively for students with the most significant cognitive disabilities (the 1% rule) |
| **What changes** | How the student accesses and demonstrates the skill | What the student is required to know or do |
| **CbD V1/V2/V3** | An accommodation framework — all three versions target the same standard | V3 is NOT a modified standard — it is a scaffolded access pathway to the same standard |

**34 CFR §300.160** states explicitly: "A State may not adopt modified academic achievement standards for any students with disabilities." The only exception is "alternate academic achievement standards," and these apply exclusively to the most significant cognitive disability population. Most students with IEPs work toward the same grade-level standards as peers — just with accommodations for access.

**The CbD access test (apply to every V3 activity):**

> "Can a student who completes this activity be said to have *demonstrated the skill* the standard requires? Or have we reduced the task to factual recall?"

- If the V3 activity still requires the student to *identify and explain* a relationship, *compare* two perspectives, or *evaluate* a source — the activity is an accommodation.
- If the V3 activity requires only *matching* or *recall* — the activity has become a modification.

**The access standard governs all differentiation decisions throughout this framework.**

### Layer 2: Text Complexity Research

**Primary framework: Common Core State Standards (CCSS) Three-Part Text Complexity Model (2010)**

The CCSS established the most widely used model for describing text complexity across three dimensions:

| Dimension | Description | CbD Application |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| **Quantitative** | Lexile level, sentence length, word frequency | Lexile band targeting (V1/V2/V3) |
| **Qualitative** | Knowledge demands, text structure, language clarity, purpose | What the reader needs to know; coherence of the text |
| **Reader and Task** | What the reader brings; what the task requires | IEP goals, AAC access, prior knowledge, response modality |

**The critical finding:** Quantitative measures (Lexile) capture only one dimension of complexity. A text with short sentences can still make enormous knowledge demands. CbD's V1/V2/V3 system addresses all three dimensions, not just Lexile.

**Lexile Framework (Stenner, Burdick, Sanford & Burdick, 2006; MetaMetrics):**

The Lexile framework measures text complexity using two variables: sentence length and word frequency. The research validates that moving within a 200L range constitutes a meaningful comprehension support; below 500L, passage length and knowledge load matter more than Lexile alone.

### Layer 3: Scaffolding Research and High-Leverage Practices

**Primary framework: Wood, Bruner & Ross (1976) — Scaffolding Theory; Vygotsky (1978) — Zone of Proximal Development; McLeskey et al. (2017) — High-Leverage Practices in Special Education**

The foundational principle underlying all CbD differentiation: **a scaffold is a temporary support that keeps the task within the learner's Zone of Proximal Development without changing the goal of the task.**

The ZPD describes the space between what a student can do independently and what they can do with support. A scaffold closes that gap temporarily — it does not permanently reduce the goal.

**High-Leverage Practices (HLPs) are a locked constant across all versions.** McLeskey et al. (2017) identified 22 HLPs in special education as the instructional practices with the strongest evidence base for students with disabilities. CbD units embed specific HLPs as the instructional mechanism for each activity type. The HLP does not change from V1 to V3 — the scaffold changes, but the practice category and research base remain constant.

*Example: An annotation activity in a nonfiction unit uses HLP 13 (Use explicit instruction) and HLP 12 (Systematically design instruction toward a specific learning goal) regardless of version. The V3 annotation may use a choice scaffold; HLP 13 and 12 are still the mechanism.*

**Browder, Ahlgrim-Delzell, Courtade, Gibbs & Flowers (2008) — Literacy for Students with Significant Disabilities:**

Specifically relevant to V3 design:
- Students with significant disabilities can and should engage with grade-level content through adapted formats
- The access pathway changes (symbol support, read-aloud, AAC response) — the content and expectation do not
- Evidence-based text access strategies include: picture-enhanced text, repeated reading with a partner, systematic vocabulary instruction embedded in the unit, and structured response opportunities with multiple modalities

**The CbD alignment:** V3 is not a simplified version — it is an accessed version with embedded vocabulary instruction.

---

### Layer 4: UDL and Access Mandate

**Primary framework: Universal Design for Learning (CAST, 2018)**

UDL Principle 1 — Multiple Means of Representation — is the research base for CbD's three-version model. UDL's core principle: barriers to learning are in the design of instruction, not in individual learners. V1 is not the "real" version — it is one access pathway. V3 is a different access pathway to the same standard.

| UDL Checkpoint | Description | CbD Implementation |
|---------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **1.1** | Offer options for perceiving information | Three text versions; Key Vocabulary instruction in V3 |
| **1.2** | Offer options for language and symbols | Symbol + text together; Fitzgerald Key color coding |
| **1.3** | Offer options for comprehension | Background knowledge activation; structured response frameworks |

## Part 2: The V1/V2/V3 Version Model

These are the authoritative definitions for all CbD products. Version labels are about the design intent of the access pathway — not a hierarchy of ability, not a judgment about student capacity.

### V1 — Grade-Level Access

**Design intent:** Full engagement with grade-level or above standards; evidence-based instructional activities at the full complexity the skill demands.

- Text targets the grade-level Lexile band (LOCKED per product line — nonfiction: 900–1050L)
- Activities are open-ended: full claim, full evidence, student-generated analysis
- Same essential question, same standard, same skill type as V2 and V3
- Same vocabulary list as V2 and V3
- Same HLPs as V2 and V3 — no simplification of the instructional practice
- Same AAC access pathway available via CAP — any V1 student who uses AAC has full communication support
- Focus skill: the ELA standard the unit is built around, practiced at full rigor

**V1 is not "general education only."** Many students with IEPs access V1 with accommodations (extended time, read-aloud). The version is an access decision, not a disability determination.

---

### V2 — Comprehension-Level Access

**Design intent:** Same theme, same focus skill, same evidence-based activities — with text complexity reduced to a 5th/6th grade comprehension level to remove the reading barrier and allow the student to demonstrate the skill without the text itself being the obstacle.

- Text targets a 5th/6th grade reading level (LOCKED per product line — nonfiction: 650–800L)
- Same essential question, same standard, same skill type as V1 and V3
- Same vocabulary list as V1 and V3
- Same HLPs — the instructional practice is the same; the entry point to the text is lower
- Sentence frames provided to support structured response; student still supplies analysis and evidence
- Focus: *one skill* — the V2 text and activity strip away secondary complexity to focus the student's energy on the single skill the standard requires
- Same AAC access pathway available via CAP

**The V2 principle:** Complexity in V1 comes from two sources — the text AND the skill. V2 removes the text complexity so the skill is the only thing the student is working on. The skill is not simplified; it is isolated.

---

### V3 — Structured Access with Embedded Vocabulary Instruction

**Design intent:** Same theme, same focus skill, same evidence-based activities — with text complexity reduced to a 3rd/4th grade reading level AND with core and fringe vocabulary actively TAUGHT within the unit, not just pre-programmed.

- Text targets a 3rd/4th grade reading level (LOCKED per product line — nonfiction: 400–550L)
- **Vocabulary is TAUGHT within the unit** — core and fringe words appear in Key Vocabulary instruction before and during the passage. This is active vocabulary instruction using the Descriptive Teaching Model and explicit instruction (HLP 12), not just a glossary.
- Same essential question, same standard, same skill type as V1 and V2
- Same vocabulary list as V1 and V2
- Same HLPs — HLP 12, 13, 14, 16 as applicable to the unit's activity types — differentiated in format only
- Activities use choice/cloze format that is compatible with AAC output, eye gaze, scanning, and alternative pencil
- All activity prompts are written to be read aloud — no V3 activity depends on the student independently reading the prompt
- Same AAC access pathway via CAP — fringe vocabulary pre-programmed to SLP 1–2 weeks before the unit

**The V3 principle:** Reading level and vocabulary access are the barriers. V3 removes both. The student is then doing the same analytical work — identifying a perspective, constructing a claim, evaluating a source — through a scaffolded format and with vocabulary support built into the lesson.

**Critical distinction:** "Core/fringe words TAUGHT within the unit" means the teacher explicitly introduces and teaches the vocabulary using evidence-based vocabulary instruction (Descriptive Teaching Model, Visual Scene Display association, repeated encounters). It does not mean a side-column definition list. The vocabulary instruction is the lesson's pre-reading component, embedded in the unit structure.

---

### AAC Access at ALL Levels

AAC access is not a V3-only feature. Communication access is present across all three versions.

| Version | AAC Access | Partner Role |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| V1 | Full CAP delivery; same core vocabulary on device; fringe pre-programmed | Partner models AAC while student reads grade-level text; full SGD/e-trans board available |
| V2 | Full CAP delivery; sentence frames support motor planning across response mode | Partner models fringe; supports device navigation; wait time protocol active |
| V3 | Full CAP delivery; vocabulary TAUGHT in unit; choice/cloze compatible with eye gaze/scanning | Partner reads aloud; explicitly introduces vocabulary; models AAC output; 5-second wait protocol |

**Hard rule:** Version assignment is NOT determined by AAC use alone. A student who uses a robust SGD and reads at grade level uses V1 with the CAP active. Version selection is a reading access decision and a vocabulary access decision — not a communication access decision. Communication access applies to every student on every version.

## Part 3: The Three Differentiation Mechanisms

### Mechanism 1: Text Complexity Reduction

Text adaptation moves from V1 → V3 through three specific tools. These tools are applied in order — not all three are required for every sentence, but all are available.

**Tool 1: Sentence decomposition**

Long, multi-clause sentences are broken into shorter sentences. The information is preserved; only the packaging changes.

| V1 | V3 |
|----|-----|
| "Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act stated that no person with a disability could be excluded from any program receiving federal money — not schools, not hospitals, not transit systems, not government offices." | "Section 504 was a law. It said people with disabilities could not be left out of programs that received money from the government. That included schools, hospitals, and transportation." |

**Hard rule:** Breaking a sentence may not remove information. Every fact, example, and piece of evidence present in V1 must appear in V3.

**Tool 2: Syntactic simplification**

Complex syntactic structures are replaced with simpler ones:
- Passive voice → active voice
- Embedded clauses → separate sentences
- Abstract noun phrases → concrete descriptions
- Academic hedging removed or simplified

**Hard rule:** Syntactic simplification may not change the author's meaning or remove the factual claim.

**Tool 3: Knowledge demand reduction + Vocabulary Instruction (V3 only)**

In V3, vocabulary and background knowledge are not assumed — they are taught:
- Key vocabulary introduced using the Descriptive Teaching Model before the passage (not as a side-bar definition)
- Fringe vocabulary embedded with meaning-making context, not just a label
- Cause-effect relationships made explicit that V1 leaves to the reader to infer
- Historical proper nouns introduced with a brief appositive

**The V3 vocabulary instruction format:**

```
KEY VOCABULARY — BEFORE YOU READ
[Word] — [Student-facing definition in plain language]
[Word] — [Student-facing definition in plain language]
Example: "pressure — when someone tries hard to make you do something you don't want to do"
```

This is followed by a brief instruction step — the partner says the word, the student finds it on their device or word card, the partner uses it in a sentence from the passage. Maximum 3–4 words per passage, all pre-programmed via CAP.

---

### Mechanism 2: Activity Scaffold Gradient

Activities are the primary place where the standard is practiced and demonstrated. The scaffold gradient governs how activity format changes across versions **without changing the skill or the HLP being used.**

| Format | Version | Structure | Example |
|--------|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Open-ended** | V1 | Blank space; full sentence expected | "Why do the government and the disability community describe the delay differently? Use evidence from the passage." |
| **Partial structure** | V2 | Sentence frame with key words; student provides evidence and analysis | "The government describes the delay as __________ because __________. The disability community describes it as __________ because __________." |
| **Choice/cloze access** | V3 | Options embedded in the task; student selects and indicates; sentence frame complete | "The government says the delay is about: (money / rights). Circle or indicate your answer. They say this because: __________ (from the passage)." |

**Hard rule — The Access Standard:** All three formats assess the same skill. The critical question: does the V3 activity require the student to demonstrate the skill, or only recall a fact? If a V3 student can select an answer without engaging the analytical skill the activity targets, the scaffold has crossed from accommodation into modification.

**HLP alignment (locked across versions):**

The HLP used in each activity type does not change across versions:

| Activity Type | HLP | What Changes V1→V3 |
|--------------|-----|---------------------|
| Close Reading / Annotation | HLP 12 (Explicit instruction) | Annotation scaffold level (teacher model → partner-guided → symbol-coded) |
| CER | HLP 14 (Scaffolded supports) | Open frame → partial frame → choice + frame |
| Perspective Comparison | HLP 13 (Learning environment) | Response format (open → structured → choice) |
| Source Analysis | HLP 12 + 14 | Source features made explicit; rating scaffold tightened |

---

### Mechanism 3: AAC Response Pathway (Applies Across All Versions)

The AAC pathway is not a V3 feature — it is a cross-version overlay that any student can access at any version level.

**What the AAC pathway provides across all versions:**
- Core response vocabulary consistent across all activities: *because, show, agree, different, same, not, true, wrong*
- Fringe vocabulary pre-programmed 1–2 weeks before the unit (via CAP to SLP) for V2 and V3
- V3: vocabulary actively taught within the unit in addition to pre-programming
- Partner read-aloud is the assumed access mode for V3; available as accommodation for V1 and V2
- All activity prompts written to be read aloud — no version depends on silent independent reading of the prompt
- All response options are speakable — a student can indicate an answer via AAC output, eye gaze, pointing, writing, or oral response

## Part 4: The Locked Constants

These elements DO NOT change across V1, V2, or V3. If a version-to-version comparison reveals any of these have changed, the product has a differentiation error.

| Element | Why It Cannot Change |
|---------|---------------------|
| **The ELA standard being addressed** | Accommodation requirement: the IEP goal targets the same standard as peers |
| **The essential question of the unit** | The intellectual purpose of the lesson is the same for all students |
| **The vocabulary list** | Same vocabulary = same motor patterns; consistent AAC pre-programming; same CAP for all versions |
| **The Top 5 Core and Fringe words** | Same priority; same communication access goal |
| **The historical/factual events described** | Every fact in V1 must appear in V3; V3 does not omit events |
| **The skill type** (Close Reading, CER, Text Structure, etc.) | The skill is the instructional target; the scaffold supports it, does not replace it |
| **The response task category** | If V1 requires a claim + evidence response, V3 requires a claim + evidence response — format scaffolded, task category locked |
| **The High-Leverage Practices (HLPs)** | HLPs are the evidence-based instructional mechanism. The scaffold changes; the practice category does not. V3 is not "less research-based instruction" — it is the same research-based instruction delivered with more support. |
| **The AAC access pathway** | Communication access is available at every version level; no version is "non-AAC" |

## Part 5: Version Assignment — Decision Logic

Version assignment is an IEP team decision, not a teacher decision made at the moment of photocopying. CbD products provide all three versions; the unit introduction guides the team through version selection.

**The four-question version assignment protocol:**

1. **What is the student's independent reading level (Lexile or equivalent)?**
   - V1: student reads within the V1 band independently, or with read-aloud as an accommodation
   - V2: student reads within the V2 band, or reads V1 with full read-aloud and vocabulary support
   - V3: student reads significantly below 500L, relies primarily on partner read-aloud, or has significant decoding needs alongside comprehension

2. **What is the vocabulary access need?**
   - V1 and V2: vocabulary pre-programmed via CAP before the unit
   - V3: vocabulary actively taught within the unit AND pre-programmed via CAP

3. **What does the student's IEP annual goal require?**
   - Most students with IEPs work toward grade-level standards with accommodations — this is V1 or V2
   - Version selection should be documented as an accommodation decision in the IEP, not as a content modification

4. **What does the educational team agree constitutes FAPE for this student?**
   - Per *Endrew F. v. Douglas County School District* (2017), FAPE requires a plan "reasonably calculated to enable a child to make progress appropriate in light of the child's circumstances." Version selection is part of that plan.

**Version assignment is not permanent.** A student who begins the year on V3 may appropriately move to V2 mid-year. The product supports this — all three versions are in the same purchase, and the vocabulary list is identical.

## Part 6: Product Line Application

### Nonfiction Reading Units (Gr 6–10)

**Lexile bands:** V1 (900–1050), V2 (650–800), V3 (400–550) — LOCKED
**Word count per passage:** V1 (550–650), V2 (450–550), V3 (330–400) — LOCKED per unit
**Activity scaffold:** V1 open-ended → V2 partial structure → V3 choice/cloze
**Vocabulary instruction:** V3 includes Descriptive Teaching Model pre-reading vocabulary instruction for 3–4 fringe words per passage; all versions have same word list
**AAC pathway:** Present in all versions via CAP + partner guidance in teacher document

**Common differentiation errors in nonfiction units:**
- Removing an event or piece of evidence from V3 to shorten the passage (forbidden)
- Changing the response task type between versions (e.g., V1 analysis, V3 identification only)
- Treating vocabulary boxes in V3 as a glossary rather than a teaching step
- Omitting the HLP label from a V3 activity section as if the practice category changed

### Fiction Anchor Texts

**Lexile bands:** TBD per unit — requires fiction differentiation session
**Text model:** The original published text is not adapted — the instructional access layer is the product
**V1/V2/V3 applies to:** Activity scaffolding and vocabulary instruction, not to the text itself
**HLPs:** Story Grammar (Spencer & Petersen 2020) + Description-First activity design constant across versions
**Emotional/mental state vocabulary:** Stays in all versions — this is the core instructional target

### Picture Book Companions (K–3)

**Version model:** Not a three-version Lexile model — read-aloud is the primary access mode for all students
**Differentiation:** Governed by IRA read-aloud structure; scaffold is in the partner questions and activity format
**AAC pathway:** Same vocabulary across all activity levels; core/fringe maintained; shared reading is the delivery model
**V3 equivalent:** Reduced sentence frame complexity + visual choice options in activities

### UFLI Phonics

**Version model:** Each lesson has a single version — differentiation is through the AAC overlay
**AAC pathway:** Communication Partner Guide governs vocabulary instruction and AAC access within each lesson
**V1/V2/V3 concept:** Not applicable within a single phonics lesson; applies across the grade band progression

## Part 7: Passage Writing — Quality Standards

### Historical and Factual Accuracy
- All events, dates, names, quotes, and statistics must be cited to a verifiable source
- Paraphrased historical content must accurately represent the source
- Quotations must be verified against the original source and not altered

### Language Standards
- Gender-neutral language throughout
- People-first OR identity-first language based on the subject's expressed preference (when known)
- Disability-affirming language: "wheelchair user" (not "wheelchair-bound"); follow the subject's expressed preference
- No inspiration framing: events described for political and historical significance, not emotional response in non-disabled observers

### Strengths-Based and Accurate Framing
- Disability rights events described as political actions, not charity
- Agency language: "Jennifer Keelan crawled up the Capitol steps" — not "Jennifer Keelan was helped to crawl"

## Part 8: QC Checklist — Pre-Build

**Gate 1: Version Definitions**
- [ ] V1 text targets grade-level Lexile band (nonfiction: 900–1050)?
- [ ] V2 text targets 5th/6th grade comprehension level (nonfiction: 650–800)?
- [ ] V3 text targets 3rd/4th grade reading level (nonfiction: 400–550)?
- [ ] V3 includes active vocabulary instruction step (Descriptive Teaching Model), not just a side-bar definition?

**Gate 2: Locked Constants**
- [ ] All factual information in V1 also present in V3?
- [ ] Essential question identical across all versions?
- [ ] Vocabulary list identical across all versions?
- [ ] Skill type (Close Reading, CER, etc.) identical across all versions?
- [ ] HLP(s) labeled and identical across all versions?
- [ ] AAC access pathway present and active in all three versions?

**Gate 3: The Access Standard**
- [ ] V3 activity requires student to demonstrate the skill (not just recall a fact)?
- [ ] V3 activity could not be completed without engaging with the text?
- [ ] Choice/cloze options require analysis, not just recognition?

**Gate 4: Activity Scaffolds**
- [ ] V1 uses open-ended response format?
- [ ] V2 uses partial sentence frame with student-supplied content?
- [ ] V3 uses choice/cloze format compatible with AAC output?
- [ ] Sentence frames present in all versions and target same grammatical structure?
- [ ] Response task category (claim+evidence, cause+effect, etc.) identical across versions?

**Gate 5: AAC Pathway**
- [ ] Vocabulary pre-programmed via CAP across all versions (V1, V2, V3)?
- [ ] V3 vocabulary instruction embedded in unit (not just pre-programmed)?
- [ ] All activity prompts written to be read aloud?
- [ ] Core response vocabulary available for all versions?
- [ ] Partner guidance addresses communication access at all three version levels?

**Gate 6: IDEA Compliance**
- [ ] Version differences documentable as accommodations (not modifications)?
- [ ] The access test passed for every V3 activity?
- [ ] Version assignment guidance references IEP team decision — not teacher-at-copy-machine?
