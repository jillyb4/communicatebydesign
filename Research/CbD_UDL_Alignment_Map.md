## Purpose

This document maps every Communicate by Design framework, product feature, and build standard to the specific UDL Guidelines 3.0 guideline and consideration numbers it implements. It serves three purposes:

1. **Research defensibility** — gives precise citations when describing CbD's instructional design in products, TPT listings, and Substack posts
2. **CAST product certification readiness** — documents alignment before pursuing formal UDL certification
3. **3.0 gap analysis** — identifies where CbD builds address or do not yet address the new 3.0 additions (bias/exclusion framing)

**Note:** CbD framework files previously cited CAST (2018) UDL Guidelines v2.2. All references should be updated to CAST (2024) v3.0. The structure is largely consistent; the key additions in 3.0 are the bias/exclusion considerations (1.3, 2.3, 2.4, 4.1 "honor", 5.4, 6.5, 7.4, 8.3, 8.4, 9.3, 9.4).

## 3.0-Specific Additions: CbD Gap Analysis

UDL 3.0 added new considerations focused on **bias, exclusion, and systems** that were not in v2.2. This section identifies where CbD already addresses them and where there is a build gap.

| 3.0 New Consideration | CbD Status | Gap / Action |
|----------------------|------------|-------------|
| **1.3** Represent diversity of perspectives and identities authentically | ✅ STRONG | Fiction pipeline is 11/31 disability-rep titles. All passage subjects are agents of their own stories. Explicitly cite 1.3 in fiction unit descriptions. |
| **2.4** Address biases in use of language and symbols | ✅ STRONG | CbD language standards (agency framing, identity-first/people-first per subject's preference, no inspiration porn). Add explicit 2.4 citation to Differentiation Framework language standards section. |
| **3.3** Cultivate multiple ways of knowing and making meaning | ⚠️ PARTIAL | CbD products use one analytical framework per product type. The "multiple ways of knowing" consideration suggests value in surfacing Indigenous, community, or non-Western knowledge frameworks alongside academic ones. *Not a current build gap for most products, but relevant to future nonfiction unit selection (e.g., Roberto Clemente unit — community knowledge).* |
| **4.1** "Vary and HONOR" response methods | ✅ STRONG | CbD's equal-access stance (AAC ≠ lesser response) is the implementation of "honor." Add this word explicitly to Communication Access Framework language. |
| **5.4** Address biases related to modes of expression and communication | ✅ STRONG — and **this is the strongest CbD→UDL 3.0 alignment point** | CbD's entire Communication Access Framework exists to remove the bias that oral response is the only valid academic communication. No other TPT product line has a framework specifically designed around 5.4. This should be stated explicitly in every product line description and in the CbD brand positioning. |
| **6.5** Challenge exclusionary practices | ✅ STRONG | CbD's Communication Partner Framework (training untrained paras) and the Participation Model (opportunity barriers) directly implement 6.5. The partner training *is* the challenge to the exclusionary practice of expecting AAC users to participate without trained support. |
| **7.4** Address biases, threats, and distractions | ✅ PARTIAL | CbD products address this at the classroom level (safe response environments). The Substack Policy & Advocacy pillar addresses it at the systems level. *Gap: no product currently includes explicit guidance on bias/threat awareness for teams working with AAC users who face "facilitation skepticism" (this is the Post #5 "Who Gets to Decide" topic — consider whether this belongs as a teacher guidance callout in future units).* |
| **8.3** Foster collaboration, interdependence, and collective learning | ✅ STRONG | The team model (SPED + para + SLP + family) is the CbD implementation of 8.3. Data roles, CAP workflow, and Session Tracker are collaborative by design. |
| **8.4** Foster belonging and community | ⚠️ EMERGING | CbD products are designed for individuals accessing curriculum. Belonging and community is present implicitly (disability-rep texts, strengths-based framing) but not yet explicitly designed as a unit objective. *Consider: should future fiction or nonfiction units include a community-connection component (e.g., "share your annotation with your AAC team")?* |
| **9.3** Promote individual and collective reflection | ⚠️ PARTIAL | Session Tracker captures data but not student reflection. *Gap: no current CbD product includes a student self-reflection component. This is an area to consider for future unit builds — particularly in fiction (emotional response) and nonfiction (perspective-taking).* |
| **9.4** Cultivate empathy and restorative practices | ⚠️ EMERGING | Disability-representation texts and agency framing cultivate empathy. Restorative practices are not yet explicitly designed into any product. *Lower priority for current build scope — flag for future consideration.* |

## Key Takeaways for CbD Positioning

### The Strongest UDL 3.0 Alignment Points

1. **Consideration 5.4** (Address biases in modes of expression) — No other TPT product line has a framework designed around this. CbD's Communication Access Framework is the only published curriculum infrastructure specifically built to remove the bias that oral response is the only valid academic communication. This is CbD's clearest UDL 3.0 differentiator.

2. **Consideration 6.5** (Challenge exclusionary practices) — Partner training as a product feature is a direct implementation of this new 3.0 consideration. CbD doesn't just provide materials — it provides the mechanism to challenge the exclusionary practice of under-training communication partners.

3. **Consideration 1.3** (Represent diversity of perspectives and identities) — The fiction pipeline's 11 disability-representation titles are the explicit product implementation of this consideration. This gives CbD a research-grounded rationale for why that list is not coincidental.
