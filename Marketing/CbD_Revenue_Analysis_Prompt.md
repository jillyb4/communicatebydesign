## What to Analyze

Work through the following questions in order. Provide a written analysis for each section, then close with a summary scorecard.

### 1. Revenue Streams — Current State
- What revenue streams are currently active vs. in development?
- Which streams have the highest margin (digital download vs. physical product vs. subscription)?
- Which streams have the highest volume potential given the target audience size?

### 2. TPT Store Potential
- Given 13 products live at current pricing ($1 to $11.95), what is a realistic monthly revenue floor, midpoint, and ceiling assuming typical TPT conversion rates for a niche SPED store?
- With 36 UFLI Phonics products launching in May 2026 and a Phase 1 bundle priced at $25, how does that change the revenue picture?
- What is the estimated total catalog value at full build-out (all product lines complete)?

### 3. Pricing Architecture — Strengths and Gaps
- Is the current pricing tiered appropriately for the audience (special educators, schools, families)?
- Are there missed pricing opportunities — site licenses, school bundles, subscription access?
- How does the nonfiction pricing ($8.95–$11.95) compare to comparable TPT products in the SPED/ELA space?

### 4. High-Leverage Growth Opportunities
- Which product line has the highest revenue-per-hour-of-production potential?
- What is the revenue upside of the AAC Trading Cards line on Etsy/Amazon vs. TPT?
- What is the Substack subscriber monetization potential (paid tier) given the content strategy and audience?
- Is there a realistic path to institutional sales (district licenses, PD contracts) and what would that require?

### 5. Revenue Risks and Structural Vulnerabilities
- What are the top 3 risks to revenue (platform dependency, audience size ceiling, production bottlenecks, etc.)?
- How concentrated is current revenue in a single platform (TPT)?
- What is the risk profile of physical products (trading cards) vs. digital?

### 6. Comparable Business Benchmarks
- Identify 2–3 comparable TPT stores or niche special education content businesses and benchmark revenue potential.
- What does a $50K/year, $100K/year, and $250K/year scenario look like for a business with this product architecture?

### 7. 12-Month Revenue Projection Framework
Build a simple projection model covering:
- Current catalog revenue (monthly run rate)
- UFLI Phonics launch uplift (May 2026)
- Fiction + Picture Book Companion additions (July 2026)
- Substack paid tier potential
- Trading card Etsy/Amazon test results (factor in low, mid, high scenarios)

Present as a table with Low / Base / High columns.

Close with a scorecard rating the following on a 1–5 scale with a one-sentence rationale for each:

End with a 3-sentence executive summary: what this business does well financially, where the biggest upside is, and the single most important action to take in the next 90 days to accelerate revenue.

## Analyst Notes

- This is a solo-founder business. Production capacity is a real constraint. Do not model growth that requires a team unless you flag it explicitly as requiring a hire.
- Products are digital downloads except AAC Trading Cards (physical + digital). Margin profiles differ significantly.
- The Substack audience is early-stage. Do not assume paid subscriber conversion until organic list growth is established.
- TPT takes a 20–45% platform fee depending on membership tier. Factor this into net revenue estimates.
- The target audience (special educators, IEP teams) skews toward individual classroom purchases, not enterprise. Institutional sales are a growth path, not a baseline.
