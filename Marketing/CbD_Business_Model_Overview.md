## Who We Are

Communicate by Design is a special education resource company founded by Jill McCardel, a special educator and advocate. Every resource in the catalog is built on one premise: the barrier to communication access is never the student. It's the system, the training, and the missing tool.

---

## The Problem We Solve

Complex communicators in school settings are not growing language at the rate they could. Not because they can't — but because three systems are failing them simultaneously.

**Curriculum.** AAC is bolted on after the fact. Teachers are handed a unit plan and then expected to reverse-engineer communication access: find symbols, write sentence starters, re-level the text, draft partner scripts. None of it was designed in from the beginning. All of it gets added after, incompletely, by someone who was never trained to do it.

**Data.** The data most teams collect doesn't measure whether language is growing. It measures compliance. "Used device in 3 of 5 trials." "Selected the correct symbol with 80% accuracy." These numbers tell you whether a student followed a direction. They tell you nothing about whether the student is combining words, initiating spontaneously, expanding meaning, or becoming a more generative communicator. Most data sheets were never designed to capture language development — and so they don't.

**IEP Goals.** Because the data is wrong, the goals are wrong. Teams write goals around the evidence they have — and the evidence they have is compliance evidence. The result is goals that target operational skills (touching the correct symbol) instead of linguistic skills (using language to mean something new). A student can meet their IEP goal every year and their language can be completely flat. The system congratulates itself. Nothing changes.

These three failures compound each other. Curriculum without built-in access generates no meaningful data. Bad data produces compliance goals. Compliance goals drive compliance instruction. And the student, who had language all along, never gets the environment they needed to grow it.

Communicate by Design exists to interrupt that cycle — at the point of instruction, with tools that any member of the team can pick up and use.

## Our Framework

All Communicate by Design products are built on the same foundation: SDI is the framework. UDL is the design philosophy. AAC is taught all day, every day, as part of everything — not as a pull-out, not as a specialist's responsibility, not as an add-on to curriculum that was designed without it.

This produces three things that look different from what most SPED resources offer:

**Curriculum with access built in from word one.** Not retrofitted. Not adapted after. Every title in the catalog arrives with vocabulary pre-selected, partner guidance embedded at the point of use, and differentiation built into the instructional architecture — not tacked on as a separate version.

**Data that captures language growth.** Not compliance. Every Communicate by Design data tool is built to capture what actually matters: communication functions, spontaneous vs. prompted language, novel word combinations, core vocabulary diversity, mean length of utterance, and communication rate over time. If a data tool can't tell you whether a student's language is developing, it isn't a language growth tool — and Communicate by Design doesn't build compliance tools.

**IEP goals that target real development.** Every goal stem in every Communicate by Design product targets a linguistic skill, not a device operation. The difference isn't subtle. "Student will use AAC device in 3 of 5 trials" measures whether a student followed a direction. "Student will spontaneously combine 2+ core vocabulary words to communicate a novel message across 3 different partners" measures whether a student's language is growing. Communicate by Design only writes the second kind.

## AT and AAC: An Ecosystem, Not a Ladder

AT, AAC, and SGDs are not a progression. They are an ecosystem.

A student who uses an e-tran board is not working toward an SGD. A student who uses PECS is not at an earlier stage than a student with a high-tech device. Every student will reach for different tools in different contexts — a symbol card at lunch, a gaze board during a lesson, an SGD for a longer communicative exchange. That is not a gap in their development. That is communication in practice.

The SGD is never the goal. The SGD is one tool in the toolbox. Communication is the goal — and every access method that serves communication is equally valid.

This shapes everything about how Communicate by Design products are built. No product assumes the student has a device. Every product works with the lowest-tech access method available: a symbol card, an alternative pencil, an e-tran board, a velcro strip. The student chooses how to demonstrate their knowledge. The product is built to receive that demonstration through any pathway.

## What We Build

All Communicate by Design products share a common instructional architecture: evidence-based frameworks, AAC-embedded access at every level, strengths-based language, and differentiation that holds the standard constant while varying the access layer.

### Product Lines

**AT/AAC Tools — IEP Team Sub-line**
Practical, ready-to-use guides and supports designed for the full IEP team — not just the SLP. Seven products live on TPT. Covers AT consideration, AAC team roles, implementation supports, and data collection tools built to measure language growth — not compliance.

**AT/AAC Tools — Family Sub-line**
A planned F0–F5 pipeline designed for families and homeschool settings. Entry point: a free resource (F0) to build trust and audience.

**Nonfiction Reading Units — Grades 6–10**
Six full units currently live on TPT. Each unit covers a historical or scientific topic through an AAC-accessible, three-version differentiation model (V1/V2/V3) — same standard, same essential question, different reading access layer. Topics include: Keiko, Radium Girls, Zitkala-Ša, The 504 Sit-In, Frances Kelsey, and The Capitol Crawl.

**Picture Book Companions — K–3**
Framework: IRA + Dialogic Reading + Aided Language Stimulation (ALS). First product: *All the Way to the Top*, timed for the ADA anniversary in July 2026.

**Fiction Anchor Texts**
SDI companion units for traditional K–12 novels already in classroom use. The product is the access layer — not a different text. First unit: *Wonder — Character Analysis*.

**UFLI Phonics**
36 lesson packets built and ready for launch (May 2026), covering the Science of Reading for ALL learners — including AAC users. Each packet integrates core and fringe AAC vocabulary directly into phonics instruction.

**Poetry Reading Units**
Access-layer model units built around the CbD-original NFMA framework (Notice / Feel / Mean / Ask). Unit 1: *What the Voice Carries*.

**AAC Trading Cards**
Physical and digital card decks organized by vocabulary scope: Starter Core (54 cards), Growing Core (170), Full Core (377), UFLI Phonics (346), and Alphabet (78). Core decks are nested. Cards are companion tools to every other product line.

## Instructional Framework

All products are governed by six evidence-based frameworks:

- **Vocabulary Selection Framework** — two-dimension classification (AAC access layer + instructional layer) applied to every word in every product
- **Differentiation Framework** — V1/V2/V3 as an accommodation model, not a modification model; standard held constant
- **Communication Access Framework** — AAC access embedded at every version level, for every AAC type, across every product line
- **Communication Partner Framework** — partner guidance built at the point of use, written for the Circle 3 paraprofessional with no prior AAC training
- **SDI Framework** — SDI components explicitly labeled and separated from accommodations in all teacher-facing materials
- **IEP Goal Ecosystem + Assessment Framework** — two-goal model (academic + AAC) per unit, observable verbs, IDEA structure, rubrics designed for the classroom

## Partnership Opportunities

**AT and AAC device manufacturers** — Every Communicate by Design product is device-agnostic by design. No product privileges one access method over another. Partnership opportunities include co-branded resources, device-agnostic implementation guides, and educator capacity-building supplements.

**School districts and special education departments** — Products are designed to drop into existing MTSS and IEP workflows without retraining. Site licensing opportunities tied to the UFLI phonics line, nonfiction units, and AT/AAC IEP Team supports — anchored to the IDEA compliance mandate that drives AT consideration.

**Special education publishers and curriculum providers** — The AAC-embedded differentiation framework and built-in communication partner guidance fills a documented gap in published SPED curricula. Licensing, co-publishing, and content integration are viable paths.

**Disability advocacy organizations** — Shared mission around access, legal compliance, and strengths-based practice. Product launches are timed to advocacy milestones including the ADA anniversary and Disability Pride Month.

**Research institutions and university programs** — Every instructional framework is grounded in peer-reviewed research with full citations. Meaningful opportunity for practitioner-research partnership, field testing, and co-authorship.
