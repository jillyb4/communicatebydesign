# Communicate by Design — Business Model Overview
**Where AT Meets Practice**
*Prepared for prospective partners and collaborators*

## Who We Are

Communicate by Design is a special education resource company founded by Jill McCardel, a special educator and advocate. The company bridges the gap between assistive technology (AT) policy and daily classroom practice — translating legal mandates, evidence-based research, and AAC frameworks into ready-to-use instructional materials that any member of an IEP team can pick up and use immediately.

Every product is built on the premise that the barrier to access is never the student — it's the system, the training, and the missing tool.

---

## The Problem We Solve

Assistive technology consideration is a federal legal requirement under IDEA, yet most IEP teams have never been trained to treat it as more than a checkbox. Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) users are consistently underserved by generic ELA materials that assume oral response, assume grade-level text access, and assume a speech-language pathologist will bridge every gap. Practitioners — special educators, paraprofessionals, BCBAs, OTs, PTs, and families — want evidence-based tools. They rarely have time to build them from scratch.

Communicate by Design exists to close that gap at the point of instruction.

---

## What We Build

All Communicate by Design products share a common instructional architecture: evidence-based frameworks, AAC-embedded access at every level, strengths-based language, and differentiation that holds the standard constant while varying the access layer. No product assumes the student has a speech-generating device. Every product works with low-tech AAC — symbol cards, e-transfer boards, alternative pencils — as the default, not the afterthought.

### Product Lines

**AT/AAC Tools — IEP Team Sub-line**
Practical, ready-to-use guides and supports designed for the full IEP team — not just the SLP. Seven products live on TPT. Eight more in the pipeline. Covers AT consideration, AAC team roles, implementation supports, and data collection tools.

**AT/AAC Tools — Family Sub-line**
A planned F0–F5 pipeline designed for families and homeschool settings. Entry point: a free resource (F0) to build trust and audience. Products will mirror IEP team supports in tone and accessibility.

**Nonfiction Reading Units — Grades 6–10**
Six full units currently live on TPT. Each unit covers a historical or scientific topic through an AAC-accessible, three-version differentiation model (V1/V2/V3) — same standard, same essential question, different reading access layer. Topics include: Keiko, Radium Girls, Zitkala-Ša, The 504 Sit-In, Frances Kelsey, and The Capitol Crawl. Pricing tiers from $8.95 to $11.95 based on passage count. A freebie (Keiko Part 1) serves as the entry-point product.

**Picture Book Companions — K–3**
A new sub-line launched in 2026. Framework: IRA + Dialogic Reading + Aided Language Stimulation (ALS). First product: *All the Way to the Top*, timed for the ADA anniversary in July 2026. Vocabulary ceiling of 15–20 words per unit. Standards-aligned to RL.K-3.3 and RL.K-3.4.

**Fiction Anchor Texts**
SDI companion units for traditional K–12 novels already in classroom use. The product is the access layer — not a different text. First unit: *Wonder — Character Analysis* (RL.6.3/7.3, Grades 3–8). Two-file architecture: main unit docx + printable packet PDF. AAC vocabulary sets at core and fringe levels.

**UFLI Phonics**
36 lesson packets built and ready for launch (May 2026), covering the Science of Reading for ALL learners — including AAC users. Each packet integrates core and fringe AAC vocabulary directly into phonics instruction. Products include individual lesson packets ($1 each), unit bundles ($5–6), and a Phase 1 bundle of Lessons 1–34 ($25).

**AAC Trading Cards**
Physical and digital card decks organized by vocabulary scope: Starter Core (54 cards), Growing Core (170), Full Core (377), UFLI Phonics (346), and Alphabet (78). Core decks are nested. Cards are companion tools to every other product line, available as standalone Etsy/Amazon products and per-product add-ons.

## Instructional Framework

All products are governed by six evidence-based frameworks developed and documented over multiple build cycles:

- **Vocabulary Selection Framework** — two-dimension classification (AAC access layer + instructional layer) applied to every word in every product
- **Differentiation Framework** — V1/V2/V3 as an accommodation model, not a modification model; standard held constant
- **Communication Access Framework** — AAC access embedded at every version level, for every AAC type, across every product line
- **Communication Partner Framework** — partner guidance built at the point of use, written for the circle-3 paraprofessional with no prior AAC training
- **SDI Framework** — SDI components explicitly labeled and separated from accommodations in all teacher-facing materials
- **IEP Goal Ecosystem + Assessment Framework** — two-goal model (academic + AAC) per unit, observable verbs, IDEA structure, rubrics designed for the classroom, not the research lab

This framework architecture is what makes Communicate by Design products coherent across grade bands, product types, and student profiles — and what makes them replicable at scale.

## Partnership Alignment Opportunities

Communicate by Design is designed for collaboration, not isolation. The framework architecture, vocabulary database (476 words and growing), and product ecosystem are built to extend — not to compete.

**AT and AAC device manufacturers** — CbD products function as independent companion resources for any AAC system. Partnership opportunities include co-branded resources, educator training supplements, and device-agnostic implementation guides.

**School districts and special education departments** — Site license and professional development opportunities tied to the UFLI phonics line, nonfiction units, and AT/AAC IEP Team supports. Products are designed to be dropped into existing MTSS and IEP workflows without retraining.

**Special education publishers and curriculum providers** — CbD's AAC-embedded differentiation framework and communication partner guidance fills a documented gap in most published SPED curricula. Licensing, co-publishing, and content integration are all viable paths.

**Disability advocacy organizations** — Shared mission around access, legal compliance, and strengths-based practice. Substack content is built to drive policy-adjacent conversations; product launches are often timed to advocacy milestones (ADA anniversary, Disability Pride Month).

**Research institutions and university programs** — The instructional frameworks are grounded in peer-reviewed research and documented with full citations. There is meaningful opportunity for practitioner-research partnership, field testing, and co-authorship.

## Where We're Headed

The near-term build cycle targets: 36 UFLI Phonics products on TPT by May 2026, the first Fiction Anchor Text unit (*Wonder*) and first Picture Book Companion (*All the Way to the Top*) by July 2026, and continued expansion of the AT/AAC Family sub-line. Longer term, the vocabulary database, communication access framework, and trading card ecosystem are designed to become a foundational layer that any new product line can grow from — not a one-off asset but a living infrastructure.

The goal is not to be the biggest special education resource store. It is to be the most coherent, the most evidence-based, and the most useful at the moment a practitioner is standing in front of a student who needs access and doesn't have it yet.
