## Full Product Description (paste into TPT description field)

**What the Voice Carries — Figurative Language in Poetry**
*A Poetry Reading Unit for Special Education · Grades 6–10*

Your students are ready for real poetry. This unit gives them access to it.

*What the Voice Carries* is a complete figurative language poetry unit built for special education classrooms, grades 6–10. Four poems — three public domain classics and one original Communicate by Design poem — taught through the NFMA framework (Notice · Feel · Mean · Ask) with three differentiated access levels, AAC support built in from the first line.

**The 4 Anchor Poems:**
- "We Wear the Mask" — Paul Laurence Dunbar (extended metaphor, tone, identity)
- "I'm Nobody! Who are you?" — Emily Dickinson (metaphor, irony, belonging)
- "The Man with the Hoe" — Edwin Markham (imagery, social commentary, labor)
- "The Words I Carry" — original Communicate by Design poem (AAC communication as metaphor; written from a perspective rarely centered in ELA)

**The NFMA Framework:**
Every poem is taught through four steps — Notice the figurative language, Feel its effect, Mean what it reveals, Ask what it raises. Every step has a V1/V2/V3 scaffold. The standard stays the same across all three versions. The access changes.

**What's Included:**
- **Unit COMPLETE** (teacher document, 16 sections) — NFMA framework overview, poem texts, differentiated activities for all 4 poems, partner guidance at point of use, IEP goal stems, rubric
- **Student Packet** (13 pages) — NFMA response pages across V1/V2/V3 for all 4 poems, sentence frames, session tracker
- **Communication Access Packet** (12 pages) — vocabulary handoff for SLP, Fitzgerald Key organization, ARASAAC symbol references, 2-week pre-programming timeline, Top 5 priority vocabulary
- **Welcome to the Product** — at-a-glance guide for the IEP team

**Standards:** RL.6.4, RL.7.4, L.5.5a–c

**AAC Access:**
Every NFMA step has a clear AAC pathway — device, e-tran board, symbol cards, gaze access, or alternative pencil. Partner guidance is written at point of use, not in a separate supplement. The Communication Access Packet gives your SLP what they need 2 weeks before Day 1.
