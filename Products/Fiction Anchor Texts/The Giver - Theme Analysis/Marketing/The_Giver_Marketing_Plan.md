## Full Description
```
The Giver: Theme Analysis
A Fiction Anchor Text Unit for Special Education · Grades 6–8

Your students are ready to read The Giver. This unit gives them access to what it means.

This is a complete theme analysis unit built for special education classrooms, grades 6–8. Whole book. AAC support designed in from the first page. The standard does not change — the access layer does.

THE CENTRAL QUESTION:
According to The Giver, what makes us fully human — and what happens when those things are taken away?

This question drives all five parts of the unit and builds toward a full theme statement by Part 5. By the time students reach the synthesis, the Theme Evidence Chart is the outline.

UNIT STRUCTURE — 5 PARTS:
Part 1: Rules of Sameness (Chapters 1–8) — What rules govern Jonas's community? What is missing, and why does that matter?
Part 2: First Memories (Chapters 9–15) — Jonas receives memories. What do they show him about what the community has lost?
Part 3: What Jonas Feels (Chapters 16–18) — Jonas begins to feel things his community cannot. How does emotion connect to theme?
Part 4: The Ending (Chapters 19–23) — What does Jonas choose? What does his choice reveal about what it means to be human?
Part 5: Whole-Book Synthesis — Students build a full theme statement supported by evidence from at least two parts of the book.

THE THEME EVIDENCE CHART:
Instead of margin annotation codes, this unit uses a cumulative Theme Evidence Chart built across all five parts. Each row captures: what happens → what it shows → how it connects to the theme. By Part 5, the chart is the outline for the synthesis response.

WHAT'S INCLUDED:

Teaching Materials (teacher document) — Research base, novel overview, all 5 parts with activities and partner guidance at point of use, communication access vocabulary table, before-reading activities, Theme Evidence Chart template with sentence frames, IEP goal stems for RL.6.2/7.2/8.2, two AAC communication goals, rubric, and answer key

Fiction Printable Packet (13 pages) — Communication Environment Setup guide · Core word symbol cards (12 words) · Fringe word symbol cards (12 words, chapter order) · Board A: Theme Evidence Vocabulary (landscape) · Board B: Emotion + Reasoning · Board C: Literary Discussion Moves · Vocabulary Map · AAC Session Tracker · Student Response Pages for all 5 parts

STANDARDS:
RL.6.2, RL.7.2, RL.8.2 — Determine a theme; analyze development; provide summary
RL.7.1 — Cite textual evidence to support analysis

AAC ACCESS:
Every part has a clear AAC pathway — device, e-tran board, symbol cards, gaze access, or alternative pencil. Partner guidance is written at point of use, not in a separate supplement. Three ready-to-laminate communication boards and symbol cards organized by chapter order are included in the Printable Packet.

Every student reads The Giver. The text does not change. The communication access tools — symbol cards, communication boards, the Theme Evidence Chart, and partner guidance — are what make the text reachable for every student in the room. The access layer varies. The expectation does not.

IEP GOAL STEMS INCLUDED FOR:
RL.6.2 / RL.7.2 / RL.8.2 — theme identification + textual evidence
AAC: building spontaneous use of theme evidence vocabulary
AAC: using communication board during literary discussion

Perfect for: resource room, inclusion support, co-teaching, SDI pull-out, summer extended school year
```
