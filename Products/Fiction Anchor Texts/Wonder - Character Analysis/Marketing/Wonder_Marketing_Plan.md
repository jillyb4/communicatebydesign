## Full Description
```
Wonder: Character Analysis
A Fiction Anchor Text Unit for Special Education · Grades 3–8

Your students are already reading Wonder. This unit gives every student in the room access to what it means.

This is a complete character analysis unit built for special education classrooms, grades 3–8. Whole book. AAC support designed in from the first page. The standard does not change — the access layer does.

THE CENTRAL QUESTION:
How does Auggie's character change across the novel, and what does that change reveal about belonging, courage, and identity?

This question drives all five parts of the unit and builds toward a full character analysis response by Part 5. Every part uses the same annotation codes — [TRAIT], [WHY], [CHANGE] — so students build evidence across the whole book.

UNIT STRUCTURE — 5 PARTS:
Part 1: Who Is Auggie? (Chapters 1–30) — Identify key character traits and the evidence that shows them
Part 2: How Others See Auggie (Chapters 31–60) — Analyze how perspective shapes character understanding
Part 3: What Auggie Feels (Chapters 61–90) — Track internal emotional change across the novel
Part 4: Auggie's Choices (Chapters 91–end) — What choices does Auggie make? What do they reveal?
Part 5: Whole-Book Character Analysis — Students build a full character analysis supported by evidence from at least two parts of the book

ANNOTATION CODES:
[TRAIT] — a character trait shown in this moment
[WHY] — evidence that explains the trait
[CHANGE] — a moment where Auggie shifts

Students mark the text with these codes as they read. By Part 5, the coded passages are the outline.

WHAT'S INCLUDED:

Teaching Materials (teacher document) — Research base, novel overview, all 5 parts with activities and partner guidance at point of use, communication access vocabulary table, annotation code guide, IEP goal stems for RL.5.3 and RL.5.6, two AAC communication goals, rubric, and answer key

Fiction Printable Packet (13 pages) — Communication Environment Setup guide · Core word symbol cards (12 words) · Fringe word symbol cards (12 words) · Board A: Character Trait Vocabulary (landscape) · Board B: Emotion + Perspective vocabulary · Board C: Literary Discussion Moves · Vocabulary Map · AAC Session Tracker · Student Response Pages for all 5 parts

STANDARDS:
RL.5.3, RL.4.3 — Describe character traits and how they develop over the course of a text
RL.5.6, RL.4.6 — Describe how a narrator's or character's point of view influences events

AAC ACCESS:
Every part has a clear AAC pathway — device, e-tran board, symbol cards, gaze access, or alternative pencil. Partner guidance is written at point of use, not in a separate supplement. Three ready-to-laminate communication boards and symbol cards for vocabulary instruction are included in the Printable Packet.

Every student reads Wonder. The text does not change. The communication access tools — symbol cards, communication boards, the annotation code system, and partner guidance — are what make the text reachable for every student in the room. The access layer varies. The expectation does not.

IEP GOAL STEMS INCLUDED FOR:
RL.5.3 / RL.4.3 — character trait identification + textual evidence
RL.5.6 / RL.4.6 — point of view and character perspective
AAC: building spontaneous use of character analysis vocabulary
AAC: using communication board during literary discussion

Perfect for: resource room, inclusion support, co-teaching, SDI pull-out, summer extended school year
```
