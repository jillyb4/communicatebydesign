## Full Description (paste into TPT)

**This is not a worksheet packet. This is the missing access layer for UFLI Foundations.**

If you have a student who uses AAC and your school uses UFLI Foundations, you've probably already hit the wall: every step of the lesson assumes the student can speak. Visual Drill? "Say the sound." Blending Drill? "Say the word." Word Chains? "Say the new word."

Your student can learn to read. They just can't show you the way the curriculum expects.

**This guide solves that problem for every single step.**

### What This Product IS

This is a comprehensive Teacher Guide paired with a Communication Partner Guide — designed so that anyone running a 1:1 UFLI lesson with a complex communicator knows exactly what to do, what to say, and what tools to use at every step.

It covers all 8 UFLI Foundations lesson steps:
1. Phonemic Awareness Warm-Up
2. Visual Drill (grapheme → phoneme)
3. Auditory Drill (phoneme → grapheme)
4. Blending Drill
5. New Concept Introduction
6. Word Work / Word Chains
7. Heart Words
8. Connected Text / Decodable Passage

For each step, you get:
- An alternative response pathway that does not require speech
- Explicit access options for EVERY AAC modality (not just "point to it")
- A step-by-step script your communication partner can follow — whether that's a para, RBT, parent, tutor, or co-teacher
- The auditory confirmation loop preserved — your student hears the correct phoneme sound every time they respond, building the internal reading voice that phonics instruction depends on

### What This Product Is NOT

This is not extra worksheets. It's not supplemental practice. It's not "UFLI but easier."

This product does not modify, simplify, or water down the UFLI lesson. The phonemic processing demand stays intact. Your student still hears, holds, and matches the phoneme — the ONLY thing that changes is the motor output required to respond. Removing the oral production barrier is not reducing rigor.

### Access Methods Supported

This guide explicitly addresses — not assumes — access for:
- **Eye gaze** (E-Trans boards, gaze boards — spacing, sizing, and field guidance included)
- **PECS** (selection-based exchange with field arrays)
- **Partner-assisted scanning** (partner says options, student signals YES)
- **Switch scanning** (single or dual switch)
- **Low-tech AAC** (communication books, choice boards, symbol cards)
- **SGD / high-tech AAC** (noted as compatible, never required)
- **Alternative pencil** (for all encoding tasks — frequency keyboard, adapted tiles, flip charts)
- **Adapted keyboard** (Clicker Writer, TD Snap keyboard pagesets)

### Research Base

This product is grounded in:
- Foley & Pollatsek (1999) — phonological processing in nonspeaking individuals

- Hudler, Hurlburt & Brock (2025) — CTD and system of least prompts for AAC learners
