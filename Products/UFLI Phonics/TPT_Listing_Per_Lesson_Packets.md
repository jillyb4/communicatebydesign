### Full Description (paste into TPT — same for all lessons, swap lesson number)

**This is a per-lesson AAC access packet for UFLI Foundations Lesson [XX]: [Phoneme] /[sound]/.**

Designed for students who are complex communicators — students who use eye gaze, PECS, partner-assisted scanning, switches, SGDs, or any combination of AAC. This packet gives your communication partner the lesson-specific materials they need to run this UFLI lesson with full access.

### What's Inside

- **Lesson info page** — phoneme, grapheme, new words, review words, heart words at a glance
- **Symbol cards** — ARASAAC pictograms for every new word, labeled core or fringe (based on AAC frequency research, not lesson-specific designation). Print, cut, and use with an e-trans board, choice array, or as PECS exchange cards.
- **Review words** — pull from the student's growing binder (built across lessons)
- **Heart words** — irregular words for this lesson with the sound/spelling mismatch flagged
- **Morphology notes** — when UFLI words are tense variations (e.g., sit/sat, has/had), flagged as a teaching moment

### What You Need First

This packet provides the lesson-specific materials. For the step-by-step scripts, access method guidance, prompting framework, and auditory confirmation loop instructions, you need the **Teacher Guide + Communication Partner Guide** (sold separately in this store). Buy the guide once — use it with every lesson.

### Symbol Card Design

Symbol cards are sized at three access levels:
- **Level 1** (partner-assisted / beginning eye gaze): ~1.75″, 3 per row, thick borders
- **Level 2** (eye gaze / direct selection): ~1.25″, 4 per row
- **Level 3** (independent device / reference): ~1″, 5 per row

All three levels are included. Choose the level that matches your student's current access. Core and fringe words are labeled based on AAC frequency research (Banajee et al., 2003; Van Tatenhove, 2009).
