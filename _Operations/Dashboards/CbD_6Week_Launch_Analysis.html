What's Working

📌 Pinterest is the fastest-moving channel

In 5 days (Apr 5–10), the Picture Book board alone generated 175 impressions, 20 pin clicks, and 4 outbound clicks to TPT. That's 80% of all outbound TPT clicks across all channels in the entire 6-week period. Pinterest is the only channel right now with a clear click-to-TPT path, and it just launched.

📚 Picture Book Companions are the surprise leader

A Friend for Henry went live April 5 and immediately pulled 13 TPT views — more than any paid nonfiction unit has received in the full 6 weeks. Ian's Walk got 7 views. These are brand new listings with no Pinterest yet and no promotion. The organic search discovery on these titles is real.

❤️ Wishlist rate is healthy

11 wishlist adds on 93 views = an 11.8% wishlist rate. Industry average for a new TPT store is closer to 3–5%. Buyers are interested — they're saving products for later. This is a demand signal that is not yet converting to purchases, likely because the store is too new and untrusted. Reviews are the unlock.

✍️ Substack content quality is on-brand and strong

5 posts published in 6 weeks is a solid cadence for launch. All three Policy Hot Take posts align with the flywheel strategy. The content is ready to distribute — the distribution step (dropping links in FB groups) is the missing activation.

🔵 Pinterest impressions are accelerating

From 0 impressions in January/February to 275 on April 8 and 271 on April 6. The curve is steep and recent. Pinterest's algorithm rewards consistent fresh pins — the daily brief infrastructure is exactly the right setup to sustain this momentum.

What Needs Attention

🔴 The funnel is broken between Facebook and everything else

1,900 Facebook views, 1 follow, 0 trackable clicks to TPT or Substack. Facebook is reaching people but there's no consistent call-to-action in the posts that routes them anywhere. Every FB post needs a Substack link drop or a direct TPT link — not as an afterthought, as the closing line.

🔴 Substack distribution has not been activated

2 subscribers after 5 posts and 179 views is a content-reaches-nobody problem, not a content-quality problem. The FB group strategy outlined in the growth workflow has not been consistently executed. The posts exist. They need to be dropped into the 7.5M-viewer groups where the questions live. This is the single highest-leverage action available right now.

🔴 Zero reviews on TPT

1 sale, 0 reviews. A new store with no reviews is asking buyers to take a risk. The wishlist rate proves interest — reviews are what convert wishlists into sales. The buyer who purchased the AAC Data Collection Forms needs a follow-up request for a review. That one review matters more than any other marketing action right now.

🟡 Nonfiction units are not finding buyers yet

Six nonfiction units have been live for up to 6 weeks with 1–5 views each and zero sales. This is a discovery problem, not a product problem. They don't have Pinterest pins yet (or very few). The Substack posts that exist (Keiko, the policy posts) don't link to the products. The seasonal content hooks (Women's History Month for Radium Girls/Frances Kelsey in March, Native American history for Zitkala-Ša) passed without a corresponding push.

🟡 Instagram is a closed loop

185 views, 52% from existing followers, 1 interaction. Instagram is not currently a discovery channel — it's a broadcast to an existing small audience. Without a carousel carousel strategy (each post ending with "link in bio → Substack"), it's not moving anyone down the funnel. Low priority for now; focus effort on Pinterest and Facebook where the reach is real.

A Friend for Henry pulling 13 views in under a week with no promotion is a signal. These titles have almost zero TPT competition (verified in pipeline research). A Friend for Henry's organic view momentum proves demand. The other 4 titles (Ian's Walk, I Talk Like a River, Emmanuel's Dream, My Friend Isabelle) need Pinterest pins this week. Picture Book board is already your best-converting board at 4 outbound clicks in 5 days.

Why: Diversifying pillar types broadens audience and begins the UFLI pipeline seeding.

Benchmark Context

A new TPT store in a niche market (AT/AAC/SPED) typically sees $0 revenue for the first 30–60 days and earns its first sale through either organic search discovery or a direct referral. The single sale in week 6 — with no social referral confirmed — is likely organic TPT search. That's a positive signal for SEO.

The business strategy VC scorecard pegged a base case of $5,350/month net at scale. Six weeks in with 22 products, $1.35 earned is not behind that trajectory — the trajectory assumes product volume, reviews, and distribution all operational simultaneously. None of those three are fully operational yet.

The strongest leading indicator is the wishlist rate (11.8%) combined with Pinterest's acceleration curve. The store is getting discovered — it just hasn't yet built the trust layer that converts discovery into purchase.
