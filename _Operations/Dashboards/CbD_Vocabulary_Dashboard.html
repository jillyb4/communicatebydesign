AAC Access Layer — What These Classifications Mean
Core Vocabulary
High-frequency words that make up the majority of what we say across contexts: go, want, more, help, stop, that, it, do, like, feel, put, look, make, say, get, come, here, now, no, yes, I, you, we — and others. Core words are likely already on a student's AAC system (SGD, e-trans board, or symbol card set). The team does not need to add them — but they do need to model them explicitly during instruction.
Team action: Model. Do not assume access means use.
Fringe Vocabulary
Topic-specific words that appear less frequently but are essential for content-area participation. Every CbD fringe word has a symbol card. Fringe words must be pre-programmed or physically available before the lesson begins — not built during it. The CAP provides the vocabulary list for each unit so the SLP or educator can prepare access.
Team action: Pre-program or print symbol cards before Day 1.
Heart Words (UFLI Only)
High-frequency irregular words from the UFLI phonics sequence — words that cannot be fully decoded by phonics rules and must be memorized. Heart words are introduced at specific UFLI lessons and are tracked by first-lesson appearance. These words receive symbol card support + phoneme sound access for complex communicators alongside phonics instruction.
Example: the, said, are, was, of, they, have, do, does
Cross-Line Words (Appears in 2+ Lines)
Words that appear in both UFLI phonics and nonfiction units — or across any two product lines — are the highest-priority vocabulary for AAC system-level support. These words bridge early phonics instruction to content-area comprehension and signal what goes on a student's permanent communication system first.
Tracked in Airtable: "Appears in Both Lines" field + Product Count ≥ 2.
Instructional Layer — How Words Are Taught
Explicit Instruction
Words that require direct, systematic teaching using the Descriptive Teaching Model. These are the words students cannot acquire incidentally from reading or conversation — especially fringe vocabulary essential to comprehension of a specific unit. Every V3 passage word receives explicit instruction.
Used in: Vocabulary section · Descriptive Teaching Model activity · Symbol cards
Background Knowledge
Words that students need to recognize but not produce — context words, proper nouns, and domain terms that anchor comprehension without being assessment targets. Built through pre-reading activity, visual scene displays, or discussion prompts rather than direct vocabulary instruction.
Used in: Pre-Reading activation · Visual Scene Displays · Partner discussion prompts
Generative Use
Words that students are expected to use in original responses — in writing, AAC output, or symbol selection. These are the target words that appear in rubrics, on response sheets, and in the Communication Access Packet vocabulary list. Core words always fall here.
Used in: Student response pages · Short answer scaffolds · Rubric language · CAP

Fitzgerald Key — Color System for AAC Symbols
CbD uses a modified Fitzgerald Key to color-code vocabulary on symbol cards, CAPs, and vocabulary maps. Colors are consistent across all product lines and correspond to word categories. Every symbol card inherits its border/background color from this system.
