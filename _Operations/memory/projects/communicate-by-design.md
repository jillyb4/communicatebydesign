## Core Philosophy: Capacity-Building Approach

**Source:** RESNA Position Paper on AT Specialists in PreK-12 Educational Settings (purchased March 6, 2026)

Capacity-building stands in contrast to the "expert model" in which AT services are performed exclusively by AT specialists. The capacity-building approach builds knowledge and skills across ALL education professionals through individual coaching, group presentations, and on-demand resources (websites, webinars, how-to videos). Some tasks still require specialized expertise, but the goal is to equip all IEP team members to take responsibility for AT within their scope of practice.

**CbD as capacity-building infrastructure:** Every resource Jill creates — Substack posts, TPT tools, community engagement — is operationalizing this philosophy. CbD is not one AT specialist doing it for everyone. CbD builds the capacity of the whole team.

## Key Rules for CbD Content

- Evidence-based first — always cite sources
- Practical over theoretical — classroom-applicable
- AT → AAC → SGD hierarchy in all content
- No jargon without explanation
- No fluff, no filler
- Do NOT position Jill as "AT Specialist" until certified
- **Strength-based language ONLY** — never "struggling," "low readers," "below grade level"
- **AAC-inclusive language** — never "point to" as sole verb; include gaze users ("select, point to, or use gaze")
- **Do NOT abbreviate "CbD" in outward-facing content** — spell out "Communicate by Design"
- **Do NOT default to "work with the SLP"** — that's the expert model. Frame as "check with your team on who manages device programming"
- **Do NOT assume the student has an SGD** — SGD is not automatic. Products must work with alternative pencil + e-trans board + symbol cards + phoneme sound access
