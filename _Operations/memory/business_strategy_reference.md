## Three Fatal Flaws Identified

1. **No enterprise sales motion.** District site licensing is aspirational, not operational. One district deal = 200 individual TPT sales worth of revenue.
2. **No recurring revenue product.** Every dollar is earned once. Zero subscription layer. Revenue is fully exposed to release-cycle volatility.
3. **Platform asset invisible to customers.** Vocabulary database (501 words), Vocabulary Explorer, and UFLI Lesson Activity are internal tools — highest-moat assets not yet generating revenue or switching costs.

## Three Pivots for Scale

### Pivot 1: District Communication Access Bundle
- Package: UFLI + AT/AAC IEP Team + 6 nonfiction = "Communication Access Starter Pack"
- Price: $499–$999 site license
- Pitch angle: IDEA compliance mandate — AT consideration is a federal legal requirement
- Channel: RESNA network + SPED director listservs + ATIA conference
- Timeline: Build before May 2026 UFLI launch — use launch as the hook

### Pivot 2: CbD Practitioner Membership (Substack → Subscription)
- Price: $12–15/month
- Includes: one new resource/month + monthly Q&A + early product access
- Free tier stays (advocacy content)
- Activation threshold: 500 free subscribers
- Revenue at 500 paid: $6,000–$9,000/month

### Pivot 3: Externalize the Vocabulary Platform
- Move Vocabulary Explorer from local HTML file to hosted web tool
- Add: IEP team vocabulary planning, product alignment tool, Communication Access Planner download
- Gate generative features: $29/year individual license or Practitioner Membership
- The database compounds: every new product adds words → more useful → more product recommendations

## AAC Trading Cards: CbD Card Collection

### Sub-Brand Name: **CbD Card Collection**
All product listings lead with: **"[Deck Name] AAC Trading Cards · CbD Card Collection"**

Example: *Starter Core AAC Trading Cards · CbD Card Collection*

### 3-Phase Launch Strategy

| Phase | What | When | Gate |
|-------|------|------|------|
| **Phase 1** | Starter Core, Growing Core, Full Core standalone decks on Etsy | NOW (from Order #82750 inventory) | Unit economics audit first |
| **Phase 2** | UFLI Phonics deck on Etsy, with Etsy callout on all TPT UFLI listings | May 2026 (UFLI launch week) | Phase 1 Etsy reviews established |
| **Phase 3** | Per-curriculum companion decks as nonfiction (→22) and fiction (→25) expand | Rolling 2026–2027 | Curriculum volume at scale |

**Full ecosystem threshold:** 22 nonfiction units + 25 fiction units + 36 UFLI = ~83 curriculum products
This is the point where "CbD Card Collection" is a complete, defensible product ecosystem.

### Market Expert Panel Key Findings

**Strongest dimension:** Repeat purchase architecture (Score: 7.5/10) — nested deck upgrade path (Starter → Growing → Full) is clinically meaningful and purchase-motivating. Per-product companion deck via `cbd_trading_cards.py` is coherent extension logic.

**Weakest dimension:** Price-to-value legibility (Score: 4.5/10) — card count is the buyer proxy metric, and Starter Core (54 cards) loses that comparison to generic sets. Fix: add a laminated reference card to every deck showing Fitzgerald Key color system + nested architecture.

**The structural problem:** Cards are designed as companion products but positioned as standalone products at the point of sale. A cold buyer has no framework to understand why 54 cards organized by Fitzgerald Key is worth more than 100 generic cards.

**Unit economics gate (MUST DO before next print run):**
- Calculate cost-per-card at 500-unit run for Starter Core, Growing Core, UFLI deck
- Compare to Etsy pricing that sustains margin after 6.5% Etsy fee + shipping
- Print-on-demand is not a viable long-term model for a 170+ card deck

### Two Etsy Listing Variants (A/B test for 30 days)
- **Variant A (clinical):** Leads with "Fitzgerald Key color coding," "core/fringe architecture," "AAC vocabulary framework" — targets SLPs, BCBAs, special educators
- **Variant B (parent):** Leads with "vocabulary cards your child can actually use," "designed for AAC users," plain language — targets families 6+ months into an AAC journey
