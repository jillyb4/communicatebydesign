# CbD Communication Partner — Operational Reference
*Added: 2026-03-29 | Full research: `Research/CbD_Communication_Partner_Framework.md`*

**Load this file when:** writing partner guidance for any CbD unit or activity, reviewing a teacher document for completeness, or building a new product line that includes AAC support.

---

## The Four Evidence-Based Partner Behaviors

Every partner guidance callout in every CbD product is built from these four behaviors.

| Behavior | What It Looks Like | Research Basis |
|---------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Model** | Point to / activate target symbol on board or device while speaking the word | Aided Language Stimulation (Goossens et al., 1989) |
| **Wait** | After asking a question, count silently to 5 before speaking again | Kent-Walsh & McNaughton (2005) — largest effect size |
| **Expand** | Accept the student's response; add 1–2 words using their system | Language expansion research; ALgS |
| **Offer Choice** | When no response after waiting, offer 2 genuine options from the text | Social Networks model (Blackstone & Berg, 2003) |

**The most impactful and most neglected:** Wait. Most partners wait fewer than 2 seconds. AAC users need 5–10 seconds for motor planning, visual scanning, and formulation. Filling that silence is the most common partner error.

---

## Partner Guidance Must Be Built-In, Not Supplemental

Partner guidance belongs inside the unit document at the point of use — not only in the welcome materials. Required locations:

- [ ] Unit introduction (AAC orientation — 1 page max)
- [ ] Vocabulary Preview (model: which words and when)
- [ ] Reading activities (read-aloud guidance; V3 embedded vocabulary)
- [ ] Response activities (wait time; choice if no response; how to expand)
- [ ] CAP page 1 (SLP + partner preparation instructions)

## Writing Partner Guidance — The Circle 3 Test

Write for a substitute paraprofessional with no AAC training who has 5 minutes before the lesson. Every guidance callout must pass:

**✗ Disposition-based (fails):** "Be patient with your student."
**✓ Behavior-specific (passes):** "After asking the question, count silently to 5. Do not nod or redirect. Wait."

**✗ Vague (fails):** "Support AAC use during this activity."
**✓ Concrete (passes):** "Point to PROTEST on the communication board when you say the word 'protest' in the passage."

**Length rule:** Partner guidance callout = 5 lines or fewer. Longer guidance is not read at the point of instruction.

## By Product Line

**Nonfiction Units:** Model during Vocabulary Preview → read V3 aloud → wait 5 sec on response tasks → expand symbol outputs → offer choices on closed activities.

**UFLI Phonics:** Model symbol for target word during blending → point to symbol + text together → AAC output = valid phoneme demonstration.

**Picture Book Companions:** Model vocabulary during pre-reading → pause at think-aloud moments → accept any modality response → expand with one additional word.

## Language Rules for Partner Guidance Text

| ✗ Do not write | ✓ Write instead |
|----------------|----------------|
| "Because the student can't speak..." | "When the student uses their device or board, that is their answer." |
| "Help struggling communicators" | "When a student selects a symbol, accept it as a complete response." |
| "Students who use AAC" as a separate group | All partner guidance applies to all students; AAC pathway is an overlay, not a subset |
| "Give them extra time" | "Wait 5 seconds. Count silently." |

**Frame AAC use as evidence of thinking, not accommodation for limitation:** "When a student selects BECAUSE + WRONG in sequence, they are building an argument. Accept this as a complete response and expand: [point to BECAUSE, WRONG, LIE] — wrong because they lied."

## QC — Pre-Publish Partner Guidance Check

- [ ] Partner guidance present in unit introduction?
- [ ] At least one callout specifies wait time with a number (5 seconds)?
- [ ] At least one callout names specific vocabulary to model?
- [ ] At least one callout explains what to do with a student AAC response?
- [ ] No callout uses disposition-based language?
- [ ] Each callout is 5 lines or fewer?
- [ ] Circle 3 test: could a para with no AAC training follow this?
