# CbD Substack Post Format — Gold Standard
*Locked April 2, 2026 — developed from AT Consideration post (Post #3)*

Use this structure for all Policy & Advocacy posts. Adapt sections 1 and 2 for other pillars.

---

## ⚠️ The Non-Negotiable: Never PD, Always Ally

The first drafts of this post read like a professional development seminar — top-down, law-first, presenter tone. That is the wrong register for every CbD Substack post.

**The test before publishing:** Read the opening. If it sounds like something you'd say at the front of a staff meeting, rewrite it.

Readers follow creators who articulate a shared frustration. Every post must position Jill as the ally of the overworked IEP team — someone who sees the same system failure they're living inside — not as someone explaining what the law says they should do.

**Never:** "The law requires teams to X."
**Always:** "Teams have been handed a mandate with almost no training on what it actually means."

The gap is always the system or the training. Never the practitioner.

---

## Structure — 4 Sections

### Opening (3–4 sentences)
- Lead with the **irony or tension** — not the legal fact or the definition
- Name the gap between what exists (technology, tools, awareness) and what the system provides
- Land the ally reframe in the final sentence
- **Formula:** *"[Students have X]. [The system is still running Y]. That's not an accusation. It's a system failure."*

> Example from Post #3:
> *"Your student lives in 2026... Their IEP team is still running a 1997 compliance script. That's not an accusation. It's a system failure."*

---

### Section 1 — The Law / The Evidence
- Pull the exact statutory or research language as a **block quote**
- Follow immediately with plain-language unpacking in short declarative sentences
- Close with systemic framing — never blame: *"Most IEP teams have never read it."*

---

### Section 2 — The Mindset Shift
- **Heading format:** "The mindset shift: [plain-language version of the problem]"
- State the narrow assumption most teams are operating under (without blaming them)
- Drop the statutory/research definition as a block quote
- Expand with concrete examples — run the full spectrum, not just the high-tech end
- Close with the **"already doing it" insight**: name what teams are already doing and call it what it is

> The contrast line that works:
> *"We've been waiting for the $5,000 solution while the $5 solution sits right in front of us."*

→ For AT/AAC framing (what AT is, how to define the spectrum): see `Research/CbD_Communication_Access_Framework.md` — AT framing section.

---

### Section 3 — What It Actually Requires
- Keep short: 3–4 paragraphs
- Name the two failure modes or barrier types without blaming the team
- **Hard contrast line:** *"A checked box with no conversation isn't [X]. A documented [Y] is."*
- One research anchor woven in as a single sentence — not a citation dump

---

### Section 4 — The Monday Tool (Cheat Sheet)
- **Heading:** "Bring this to your next meeting" (or equivalent)
- Lead with: *"You don't need a [credential/certification] to have this conversation."*
- Add screenshot prompt ABOVE the questions:
  > *"Screenshot this. These are the [X] questions that turn [problem] into [solution]."*
- 3 questions max. Each = **bold question** + 1 sentence of context underneath
- Close: *"The [team] already in that room knows this student. That is exactly the team the law had in mind."*

---

### Close
1. **Follow driver** (1 sentence): *"I'm building a full library of tools for [audience] who want to [goal]. Follow along to get the next one."*
2. **AI disclosure** (required, on its own line):
   *"I use AT in my work — including AI writing tools. Every idea and professional position comes from my own experience. AI helps me get it out of my head and onto the page."*

## Ally Language — Reference

| ❌ PD Seminar | ✅ Ally Framing |
|---|---|
| "The law requires teams to…" | "IEP teams have been handed a legal mandate with almost no training on what it means." |
| "Consider the following definition…" | "Here's where most teams get stuck: they're working with a much narrower definition than the law uses." |
| "Research identifies two barrier types…" | "Researchers who study why AT fails consistently identify two barriers…" |
| Ends with a sentiment | Ends with a direct follow driver + tool promise |

**Phrases that work:**
- *"It's time to stop apologizing for that system and start using the law the way it was intended."*
- *"That's not an accusation. It's a system failure."*
- *"We've been waiting for the $5,000 solution while the $5 solution sits right in front of us."*
- *"You don't need a [certification] to have this conversation."*
- *"The team already in that room knows this student. That is exactly the team the law had in mind."*

## The Shareable Asset Rule
Every post needs at least one **screenshot-worthy asset**:
- 3-question cheat sheet (primary)
- Hard-contrast line ("A checked box isn't X. A documented Y is.")
- Single pull-quote that distills the post in one sentence
- **Photo overlay quote** (secondary shareable — make in Canva for every Policy post)

> Post #3 photo overlay: *"You don't need a specialist to tell you what your student needs. You need the courage to name what you're already seeing."*
